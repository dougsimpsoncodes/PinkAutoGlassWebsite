## CRITICAL: Conversion Tracking Is Broken

Microsoft Ads conversion data is **unreliable and must be fixed before any optimization decisions**. Evidence:

| Search Term | Clicks | Reported Conversions | Problem |
|-------------|--------|---------------------|---------|
| windshield replacement fort collins colorado | 8 | 84 | 10.5 conv/click — impossible |
| windshield chip repair highlands ranch co | 8 | 32 | 4 conv/click — impossible |
| molbile windshield replacement | 8 | 32 | 4 conv/click — impossible |
| windshield replacement nearinterlaken ny | 8 | 16 | Out of area AND impossible ratio |
| lakeside autobrokers | 7 | 7 | Not even an auto glass search |

**Root cause (likely):** The UET tag conversion goal is set to count **"Every"** conversion instead of **"Unique."** This means every page load or event re-fire counts as a separate conversion.

**Fix:** In Microsoft Ads > Tools > Conversion Goals:
1. Find the active conversion goal
2. Change counting from "Every" to "Unique"
3. Verify the UET tag fires only on actual conversion events (form submit, phone click), NOT page loads

## CTR Problem: 0.49% vs Industry Benchmarks

Microsoft Ads CTR for auto glass should be 2-4%. At 0.49%, the ads are showing to the wrong people or the copy isn't resonating.

**Likely causes:**
1. Broad match keywords matching irrelevant queries (confirmed by search term data)
2. Geographic targeting too wide (confirmed by out-of-area searches)
3. Ad copy may not match search intent
4. Single campaign structure limits targeting precision

**Recommendations:**
- After fixing geo + negatives, CTR should improve to 1.5-2.5%
- Consider splitting into themed ad groups (repair vs replacement, by location)
- Review ad copy for Denver-specific messaging
