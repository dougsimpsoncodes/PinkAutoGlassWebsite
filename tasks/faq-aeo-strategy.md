**Created:** 2026-02-28
**Purpose:** Concrete FAQ content strategy for pinkautoglass.com and all 13 satellite sites, optimized for AI engine citation (AEO) and local SEO.

## 1. Why FAQs Matter Right Now (The AEO Shift)

Traditional SEO: get Google to rank your page.
AEO (Answer Engine Optimization): get ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, and Gemini to cite your page when someone asks a question.

These are different optimization targets, but FAQ content with FAQPage schema serves both.

Key data points from current research:
- Pages with FAQPage schema are ~3.2x more likely to appear in Google AI Overviews
- FAQPage schema produces 35-50% higher citation rates in AI engines than unstructured content
- Structured FAQ content with schema generates 40-60% higher citation rates vs. unstructured FAQ content
- Only 12% of domains use structured data — this is a real competitive gap in local auto glass

**Important Google rich results caveat (2023, still in effect as of 2026):**
Google no longer shows the expanded FAQ dropdown rich result in traditional search for most sites — that feature is restricted to government and health sites. Do NOT implement FAQs chasing that feature. Implement them for:
1. Google AI Overviews (high value, growing)
2. ChatGPT and Perplexity citation (growing rapidly)
3. Voice search
4. On-page user experience and conversion
5. Bing and DuckDuckGo (still show FAQ rich results)

## 2. AEO Content Architecture Rules

These are non-negotiable. Every FAQ answer must follow this structure.

### The BLUF Rule (Bottom Line Up Front)
Lead every answer with the direct answer. No wind-up. No "Great question." No "There are many factors to consider."

```
WRONG:
"The cost of windshield replacement depends on many variables
including your vehicle make, model, year, glass type, and
whether you have insurance coverage..."

CORRECT:
"Windshield replacement in Denver typically costs $200-$400
without insurance, or $0 out-of-pocket if you have
comprehensive coverage. Pink Auto Glass handles your
insurance claim for you."
```

### Ideal Answer Length: 40-75 Words
- Under 30 words: too thin, AI engines skip it as insufficient
- 40-75 words: sweet spot for complete extraction without truncation
- Over 80 words: AI engines truncate or paraphrase, losing your specifics

### Sentence Structure
- Sentence 1: The direct answer with a number, fact, or clear position
- Sentence 2: One clarifying detail or condition
- Sentence 3 (optional): Local specificity or next-step CTA

### Maximum Sentence Length: 20 words
Long sentences confuse LLM parsers. Short sentences get extracted cleanly.

## 3. The Top 28 FAQ Questions for Pink Auto Glass

Grouped by topic. Use these as the core set, then vary them per satellite site (see Section 6).

### Group A: Repair vs. Replacement

**Q1: Can a cracked windshield be repaired, or does it need full replacement?**
A chip or crack smaller than a dollar bill (6 inches) can usually be repaired. Damage in the driver's direct line of sight, or cracks longer than 6 inches, require full replacement. A Pink Auto Glass technician will assess your damage for free before recommending any service.

**Q2: How do I know if my windshield chip will spread?**
Temperature changes, road vibration, and pressure from washing your car all cause chips to spread. A chip left untreated for more than 2-3 days in Colorado or Arizona weather has a high chance of cracking further. Repair is fastest and cheapest when done immediately.

**Q3: Is windshield chip repair permanent?**
Chip repair fills the damage with UV-cured resin that bonds to the glass and restores structural strength. It is permanent in the sense that it prevents spreading. The repair will likely remain visible under certain lighting, but it will not worsen or spread.

### Group B: Cost and Insurance

**Q4: How much does windshield replacement cost in Denver?**
Without insurance, windshield replacement in Denver typically costs $200-$450 depending on your vehicle make, model, and whether it has ADAS sensors. With comprehensive insurance coverage, Colorado law (CRS 10-4-613) requires insurers to offer zero-deductible glass coverage, meaning most Denver drivers pay $0 out-of-pocket.

**Q5: How much does windshield replacement cost in Phoenix?**
Without insurance, windshield replacement in Phoenix typically costs $200-$400. Arizona is a zero-deductible state for glass claims — your insurance company cannot raise your rates for filing a windshield claim, and most comprehensive policies cover full replacement at no cost to you.

**Q6: Does insurance cover windshield replacement?**
Yes, if you have comprehensive coverage. Most insurance policies cover windshield repair or replacement with no deductible. Pink Auto Glass handles the entire insurance claim for you — you call us, we contact your insurer, and we schedule your service. Most customers pay nothing out-of-pocket.

**Q7: Will filing a windshield claim raise my insurance rates?**
In Colorado and Arizona, filing a glass claim under your comprehensive coverage cannot legally raise your rates. These states treat windshield claims as no-fault events. Your premium is protected.

**Q8: How much does chip repair cost without insurance?**
Windshield chip repair without insurance typically costs $50-$100 for the first chip, with $10-$25 per additional chip. If you have comprehensive insurance coverage, chip repair is usually free — your insurer covers it with no deductible.

### Group C: Mobile Service

**Q9: What is mobile windshield replacement?**
Mobile windshield replacement means a certified technician comes to your location — your home, office, or parking lot — and replaces your windshield on-site. You do not need to drive to a shop. Pink Auto Glass provides mobile service across the Denver and Phoenix metro areas at no extra charge.

**Q10: How does mobile windshield service work?**
You call or request a quote online, pick a time and location that works for you, and a certified technician arrives with your replacement glass. The job takes 60-90 minutes. You then wait 1-2 hours before driving so the adhesive cures properly. Total interruption to your day: under 2 hours.

**Q11: Can you come to my workplace for windshield replacement?**
Yes. Pink Auto Glass can come to any parking lot, office building, or home in the Denver and Phoenix metro areas. Mobile service is included at no extra charge. Many customers have their windshield replaced during work hours without missing any time from the office.

**Q12: How soon can you come out for same-day windshield replacement?**
In most cases, Pink Auto Glass can schedule same-day service for requests made before noon. We serve the full Denver metro area including Aurora, Littleton, Englewood, Lakewood, and Westminster. Call us and we will give you an exact availability window.

### Group D: ADAS and Safety

**Q13: What is ADAS calibration and do I need it after windshield replacement?**
ADAS (Advanced Driver Assistance Systems) includes lane departure warning, forward collision alerts, and automatic emergency braking — features that use a camera mounted to your windshield. When you replace your windshield, that camera must be recalibrated to factory specifications. Without recalibration, these safety systems may not function correctly.

**Q14: How much does ADAS calibration cost?**
ADAS calibration typically costs $250-$600 depending on your vehicle. Most comprehensive insurance policies cover calibration when it is part of a windshield replacement claim. Pink Auto Glass will tell you upfront whether your vehicle requires calibration and whether your insurance covers it.

**Q15: Does every vehicle need ADAS calibration after windshield replacement?**
No. Only vehicles with camera-based driver assistance systems require recalibration. Roughly 90% of vehicles manufactured after 2018 have at least one ADAS feature. Older vehicles with no driver-assist cameras do not require calibration. Your technician will confirm before the job starts.

**Q16: How long does ADAS calibration take?**
Static calibration takes 30-45 minutes in a controlled environment. Dynamic calibration requires driving the vehicle at highway speeds for 20-30 minutes while a connected computer resets the camera. Some vehicles require both (dual calibration), which takes 60-90 minutes total.

### Group E: Glass Quality

**Q17: Should I choose OEM or aftermarket windshield glass?**
OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturer) glass is identical to what came with your vehicle from the factory and guarantees a perfect fit with your ADAS camera and seals. Aftermarket glass costs less but may not meet the same precision tolerances. For vehicles with ADAS systems, OEM glass is strongly recommended. Pink Auto Glass can provide both options.

**Q18: Does the glass quality affect ADAS calibration?**
Yes. Low-quality aftermarket glass can cause calibration errors or ongoing ADAS malfunctions because the optical clarity and exact placement of the glass affects how the camera reads the road. Using OEM or OEE (Original Equipment Equivalent) glass reduces calibration problems significantly.

### Group F: Process and Timing

**Q19: How long does windshield replacement take?**
A windshield replacement takes 60-90 minutes for the physical installation. After that, you need to wait 1-2 hours before driving to allow the urethane adhesive to fully cure and create a proper seal. Total time from start to drive-away is typically 2-3 hours.

**Q20: How long after windshield replacement can I drive?**
Safe drive-away time is typically 1 hour for modern fast-cure urethane adhesives under normal temperature conditions. In cold weather (below 40°F), allow at least 2 hours. Your technician will give you the specific drive-away time based on the adhesive used and weather conditions.

**Q21: Can I drive with a cracked windshield?**
Technically yes, but it is unsafe and illegal in most states if the crack impairs driver visibility. A cracked windshield also has compromised structural integrity — in a rollover accident, the windshield contributes 30-45% of a vehicle's roof crush resistance. Colorado and Arizona both have laws requiring unobstructed driver sightlines.

**Q22: How do I get a windshield replacement quote?**
You can get a free quote from Pink Auto Glass by calling us directly or filling out our online form. We need your vehicle year, make, model, and zip code. We will confirm your insurance coverage and give you an exact price before scheduling anything.

### Group H: Trust and Credentials

**Q26: Are your technicians certified?**
Yes. Pink Auto Glass technicians are certified through the Auto Glass Safety Council (AGSC) and trained on ADAS recalibration procedures. We use industry-standard urethane adhesives and follow all manufacturer installation guidelines to maintain your vehicle's warranty coverage.

**Q27: Is mobile windshield replacement as good as going to a shop?**
Yes. The materials and techniques used for mobile replacement are identical to those used in a shop. The same OEM or OEE glass, the same urethane adhesive, the same safety standards. The only difference is that our certified technician comes to you.

**Q28: Do you offer a warranty on windshield replacement?**
Yes. Pink Auto Glass backs every installation with a lifetime warranty against defects in materials and workmanship — including leaks, wind noise, and glass defects. If you experience any issue with your installation, we will come back and fix it at no charge.

### Schema Rules
- The `name` field: exact question text as it appears on the page
- The `text` field: full answer text, can include HTML tags (`<strong>`, `<a>`, `<ul>`, `<li>`, `<p>`) — use sparingly
- Only one `FAQPage` type per page
- Every Q&A in the schema must be visible on the page — no hidden FAQ schema
- Do not mark up the same FAQ on multiple pages (one canonical location per FAQ)
- Validate every implementation at https://search.google.com/test/rich-results

### Where to Put the Schema in Next.js
In the page component, add it inside a `<script>` tag using `dangerouslySetInnerHTML`:

```tsx
// In your FAQ page or component
export default function FAQSection() {
  const faqSchema = {
    "@context": "https://schema.org",
    "@type": "FAQPage",
    "mainEntity": [
      // ... your questions
    ]
  };

  return (
    <>
      <script
        type="application/ld+json"
        dangerouslySetInnerHTML={{ __html: JSON.stringify(faqSchema) }}
      />
      {/* Your FAQ UI */}
    </>
  );
}
```

## 5. FAQ HTML Structure for Maximum AI Crawlability

The visual structure on the page matters as much as the schema. AI crawlers read semantic HTML.

### Use This Pattern

```html
<section aria-label="Frequently Asked Questions">
  <h2>Frequently Asked Questions</h2>

  <div itemscope itemprop="mainEntity" itemtype="https://schema.org/Question">
    <h3 itemprop="name">Can a cracked windshield be repaired, or does it need full replacement?</h3>
    <div itemscope itemprop="acceptedAnswer" itemtype="https://schema.org/Answer">
      <p itemprop="text">
        A chip or crack smaller than a dollar bill (6 inches) can usually be repaired.
        Damage in the driver's direct line of sight, or cracks longer than 6 inches,
        require full replacement. A Pink Auto Glass technician will assess your damage
        for free before recommending any service.
      </p>
    </div>
  </div>

  <!-- repeat for each question -->
</section>
```

### Key HTML Signals
- Wrap the section in `<section>` with an `aria-label`
- Use `<h2>` for the "Frequently Asked Questions" heading
- Use `<h3>` for each individual question (not `<h4>` or `<p>`)
- Answer text goes in `<p>` tags, not `<div>` tags
- Do not hide questions behind JavaScript accordions that require a click to expand — Google and AI crawlers may not execute JavaScript for FAQ content. Use CSS accordion if you must, with content in the DOM (not `display:none`)
- If using a JS accordion, ensure the text is in the DOM and not conditionally rendered

## 6. Varying FAQs Per Satellite Site (Duplicate Content Prevention)

The 70/30 rule applies: at least 70% of FAQ content must be unique per domain. Swapping only a city name in a template is not enough.

### The Three-Tier Variation System

**Tier 1 — Universal FAQs (same across all sites, ~30% of FAQ set)**
These cover topics that do not vary by location:
- Repair vs. replacement decision
- ADAS calibration (what it is, how long it takes)
- OEM vs. aftermarket glass
- How mobile service works (mechanically)
- Warranty

**Tier 2 — State-specific FAQs (vary by Colorado vs. Arizona, ~30% of FAQ set)**
These cover state law and insurance rules that differ between the two markets:

*Colorado / Denver sites:*
- Reference CRS 10-4-613 (zero-deductible glass coverage requirement)
- Note Colorado's hail season (April-September) creating chip and crack volume
- Reference Front Range / altitude temperature swings causing chips to spread faster

*Arizona / Phoenix sites:*
- Reference Arizona's no-fault glass claim law (rates cannot increase)
- Note Arizona's UV/heat extremes as a cracking risk factor
- Note monsoon season debris as a common chip cause

**Tier 3 — City/Neighborhood-specific FAQs (unique per satellite domain, ~40% of FAQ set)**
These are the questions that make each satellite site genuinely unique.

Example variations:

**windshieldchiprepairphoenix.com:**
- "Do you repair windshield chips in Phoenix, AZ?"
  Answer mentions Phoenix specifically, 85001-85099 zip codes, Camelback, Tempe, Scottsdale nearby
- "How long does windshield chip repair take in Phoenix?"
  Answer: mentions Arizona heat cure time, UV cure vs. indoor light source

**windshieldcostphoenix.com:**
- "What is the average windshield replacement cost in Phoenix, AZ?"
  Answer: Phoenix-specific price range, Arizona insurance law, no cash-back upsell language
- "Is windshield replacement covered by insurance in Phoenix?"
  Answer: Lead with Arizona zero-deductible law, include State Farm / USAA / Progressive as common carriers in the area

**mobilewindshieldphoenix.com:**
- "Do you offer mobile windshield replacement in Phoenix?"
  Answer: List Scottsdale, Tempe, Mesa, Chandler, Gilbert, Glendale coverage
- "Can you come to Scottsdale for mobile windshield replacement?"
  Answer: Confirms Scottsdale coverage, mentions Paradise Valley, North Scottsdale, Old Town

**Denver satellite sites:**
- "Do you replace windshields in Aurora, CO?"
  Answer: Confirms Aurora (80010-80019), mentions Centrepoint, Southlands, DTC proximity
- "How does hail damage affect my windshield?"
  Answer: Colorado-specific, mentions ADAS calibration after hail-damaged windshield replacement, insurance claim process

## 7. Question Sourcing — How to Find More Real Questions

### Priority Sources (use in this order)

1. **Google "People Also Ask" boxes**
   Search "windshield replacement Denver" or "windshield chip repair Phoenix" and screenshot every PAA question. These are real queries with real search volume.

2. **Google Search autocomplete**
   Type "windshield replacement" and note every autocomplete suggestion. Then type "windshield chip" and do the same. These are what people are actually typing.

3. **Google Search Console (your own data)**
   In GSC, filter by queries containing "windshield" or "auto glass." Every query that showed an impression for your site is a real question a real user asked. The ones with high impressions and no clicks need to be answered in FAQ content.

4. **Answer the Public (answerthepublic.com)**
   Free tool. Enter "windshield replacement" and it generates every question format (who, what, when, where, why, how, which, will, can, are) Google has indexed.

5. **Reddit and Quora**
   Search r/Denver, r/phoenix, r/personalfinance for "windshield" threads. These surface real objections and confusion that formal keyword tools miss. Common Quora questions about auto glass are high-value FAQ content.

6. **Your own inbound calls and form submissions**
   Whatever your customers ask before booking is the most valuable FAQ source you have. Keep a running list of the 10 questions your team answers most often on phone calls. Those become FAQs.

## 8. Prioritized Implementation Order

Do these in order. Do not implement all 13 satellite sites at once — start with the highest-traffic domains.

### Phase 1: pinkautoglass.com (main site)
- Add full FAQ section (Questions 1-22 from Section 3, both Denver and Phoenix versions)
- Implement FAQPage JSON-LD schema
- Use proper HTML structure from Section 5

### Phase 2: Top 3 satellite sites by traffic
- Identify from Google Search Console which satellite domains have the most impressions
- Build site-specific FAQ sets using the Tier 1/2/3 framework in Section 6
- Each satellite site should have 8-12 FAQ questions, not all 28

### Phase 3: Remaining satellite sites
- Roll out systematically, one at a time
- Validate schema on each with Google's Rich Results Test after launch

### What Not to Do
- Do not copy-paste the same FAQ to all 13 domains — that is duplicate content and will suppress all of them
- Do not use "How much does windshield replacement cost in [CITY]?" as your only location signal — vary the question framing too
- Do not implement FAQ schema on pages where the FAQ is hidden in a collapsed accordion that requires JavaScript to reveal
