What is broken or incomplete

  • On-site analytics cannot be fully trusted.
  • Lead attribution is patchy and too many records are unattributed.
  • City/state data is inconsistent even when zip and revenue exist.
  • Call demand is only partially connected back to web sessions and campaigns.

Why it matters

  • Google Ads may be optimizing toward soft or partially observed conversions.
  • Denver-specific ROAS cannot be stated with confidence yet.
  • CRO testing is riskier when the baseline instrumentation is this weak.

Slide 8

Top 7 actions, ranked by impact

  1. Fix measurement first. Audit GA4/GTM/session tracking, bounce logic, pageview capture, and lead hidden fields.
  2. Lock down phone attribution. Audit call extensions, GBP calls, desktop/manual dial paths, and dynamic number insertion.
  3. Tighten Google Ads conversion definitions. Make sure bidding is optimizing on hard leads, not soft proxy actions.
  4. Rebuild the Denver SEO surface. Upgrade /locations/denver-co for local intent, local proof, and Denver-specific relevance.
  1. Protect the homepage conversion path. Keep the urgent-user funnel fast, mobile-first, and low-friction.
  2. Validate PMax before scaling it. Check brand cannibalization and offline quality before trusting the low CPA.
  3. Use Denver service pages as traffic sinks. Improve rankings and internal linking into windshield replacement, repair, insurance, and Denver location pages.
